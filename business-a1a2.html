What Can You Do at A1/A2 Business Level?

At A1/A2, learners can exchange basic information about themselves and their workplace — job title, department, company size, working hours, salary, and simple tasks. They can understand short notices, basic emails, and name cards. Vocabulary covers job titles, departments, times, prices, and office objects.

💼 Business Text · A1/A2 Topic: First Day at Work
First Day at the Office
A new employee arrives at a technology company in London and meets her colleagues for the first time.

Today is Monday, 3 March. It is Sarah Mitchell's first day at work. She is a new Marketing Assistant at GlobalBridge Ltd, a small technology company in central London. The company has 45 employees. The office is on the 4th floor of a building on King Street.

Sarah arrives at the office at 8:45 am. The working day starts at 9:00 am and finishes at 5:30 pm. There is a lunch break from 1:00 pm to 2:00 pm.

At reception, she meets Mr James Okafor, the Office Manager. He gives her a visitor badge, a computer password, and a copy of the office rules. He shows her the kitchen, the meeting rooms, and the fire exit.

Sarah's desk is in the Marketing Department, on the right side of the office. Her manager is Ms Diana Holt, the Head of Marketing. Diana's office is in the corner, next to the window.

📞 Dialogue — Sarah Meets Her Manager
Diana:Good morning, Sarah! Welcome to GlobalBridge. I'm Diana Holt, Head of Marketing. Nice to meet you.
Sarah:Good morning, Ms Holt. Thank you — I'm very happy to be here.
Diana:Please call me Diana. Let me show you your desk. Do you have any questions?
Sarah:Yes — what time is the team meeting?
Diana:Every Monday at 10:00 am in Meeting Room B. It usually takes about 45 minutes.
Sarah:And where is the printer?
Diana:Next to the photocopier, near the window. Your monthly salary is £2,400. You get paid on the last Friday of every month.

After the meeting, Sarah reads a short welcome email from the HR Department.

📧 Email from HR Department

To: sarah.mitchell@globalbridge.co.uk
From: hr@globalbridge.co.uk
Subject: Welcome to GlobalBridge Ltd

Dear Sarah,
Welcome to the team! Your employee number is GB-4471. Your desk phone extension is 247. The staff canteen is on the 2nd floor. Lunch costs £4.50. The IT helpdesk number is 0800 123 456.

Best regards,
HR Department · GlobalBridge Ltd

Sarah feels very positive about her new job. She says: "GlobalBridge is a great company. Everyone is very friendly and helpful. I think I will really enjoy working here."

📖 Key Business Vocabulary — A1/A2
employee — a person who works for a company
manager — a person who leads a team
department — a section of a company (e.g. Marketing, HR)
reception — the front entrance desk of a company
salary — the fixed money you earn each month
extension — a short internal phone number in an office
canteen — a cafeteria inside a company building
HR (Human Resources) — the department that manages employees

Business Tip · A1/A2
Professional Protocol: In a British workplace, always use titles (Mr, Ms, Dr) until you are invited to use first names. Knowing a person's job title tells you their level of authority — and their department tells you what they are responsible for.
