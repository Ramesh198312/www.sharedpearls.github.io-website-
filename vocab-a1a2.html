What is CEFR A1 / A2?

The Common European Framework of Reference (CEFR) is an international standard used to describe language ability. A1 (Beginner) learners can understand and use very basic expressions to introduce themselves and interact simply. A2 (Elementary) learners can communicate in routine tasks, describe their background, immediate environment, and matters of immediate need. At this level, vocabulary focuses on concrete, everyday words — family, food, colours, numbers, jobs, and simple actions.

Core Vocabulary — 20 Essential Words

Study each word carefully. Notice the part of speech, definition, and two example sentences. The first example is everyday; the second is slightly more formal.

happy
adjective
Feeling pleasure or joy; contented and satisfied.
"I am very happy to meet you today."
"She felt happy when she received the good news."
buy
verb
To get something by paying money for it.
"I want to buy a new bag."
"He bought a dictionary to improve his vocabulary."
family
noun
A group of people related by blood or marriage.
"My family has four people: mum, dad, my sister and me."
"She lives with her family in a small town."
work
verb / noun
To do a job or task; a place or activity where you earn money.
"I work in a school."
"He goes to work every morning by bus."
hungry
adjective
Feeling the need to eat; having an empty stomach.
"I am hungry. Can we eat now?"
"The children were hungry after their long walk."
travel
verb / noun
To go from one place to another, especially over a distance.
"I love to travel to new countries."
"She travels by train to the city every week."
friend
noun
A person you know well and like very much.
"My best friend is called Sara."
"He made many friends at his new school."
open
verb / adjective
To move something so it is not closed; not shut.
"Please open the window. It is very hot."
"The shop is open from 9 am to 6 pm."
beautiful
adjective
Very pleasing to look at; attractive in appearance.
"What a beautiful flower!"
"They live in a beautiful house by the sea."
speak
verb
To say words aloud; to talk to someone.
"Do you speak English?"
"She speaks three languages: English, French and Arabic."
tired
adjective
Feeling the need to rest or sleep; not having energy.
"I am very tired after work today."
"He looked tired after the long journey."
ask
verb
To say something as a question; to request information.
"Can I ask you a question?"
"She asked the teacher for help with the exercise."
need
verb / noun
To require something that is necessary or important.
"I need to drink some water."
"Do you need any help with your homework?"
early
adjective / adverb
Before the usual or expected time; near the beginning.
"I wake up early every morning."
"She arrived early to prepare for the meeting."
money
noun
Coins and notes used to buy things; currency.
"I do not have enough money to buy that bag."
"He saves money every month in a bank account."
understand
verb
To know what something means; to get the meaning of.
"I don't understand this word. Can you help?"
"Do you understand the instructions for the exercise?"
busy
adjective
Having a lot to do; occupied with work or activities.
"Sorry, I can't talk now. I am very busy."
"The office is always busy on Monday mornings."
place
noun
A particular area, location, or position.
"This is a nice place to have lunch."
"The library is a quiet place to study."
learn
verb
To get knowledge or a skill by studying or practising.
"I want to learn English quickly."
"She is learning to drive a car this month."
important
adjective
Having great value, significance or meaning; necessary.
"Water is important for our health."
"It is important to arrive on time for a job interview."

Study Tip

The best way to learn vocabulary at A1/A2 is to connect words to things around you. Point to objects in your home and say the word aloud. Make short sentences about your daily life.
